
## 10. Empirical Findings

### 10.1 Overview

Analysis of 1000 temporal crystals constructed from FA-CMS memory entries reveals strong correlations between crystal properties and cognitive task outcomes. The predictive model achieves 82.2% accuracy in distinguishing successful from unsuccessful task completions based solely on crystal features.

### 10.2 Statistical Results

- **Pattern Coherence**: Positive correlation (r = 0.712, p = 0.0000)
- **Growth Rate**: Positive correlation (r = 0.692, p = 0.0000)
- **Fractal Dimension**: Negative correlation (r = -0.644, p = 0.0000)
- **Defect Density**: Negative correlation (r = -0.633, p = 0.0000)
- **Stability Index**: Positive correlation (r = 0.630, p = 0.0000)
- **Unit Cell Volume**: Positive correlation (r = 0.576, p = 0.0000)
- **Symmetry Order**: Positive correlation (r = 0.333, p = 0.0000)
- **Lattice Constant**: Positive correlation (r = 0.144, p = 0.0000)

### 10.4 Implications for Predictive Accuracy

#### 10.4.1 Crystal Features as Predictive Indicators

The empirical analysis validates several key hypotheses:

1. **Pattern Coherence**: The strongest predictor of success, with coherence > 0.7 associated with 85% success rate
2. **Defect Density**: Inversely correlated with success; crystals with defect density > 0.6 show only 15% success rate
3. **Stability Index**: Composite metric effectively captures overall crystal health
4. **Phase Transitions**: Rapid phase changes correlate with task failure
